Tachograph symbols: digital icon meanings explained

Truck Data TechnologyUpdated May 20267 min read

Understanding tachograph symbols is essential for drivers' hours compliance. The icons tell you what activity is being recorded and whether you are on a lawful break, working, or driving.

If you have ever asked "what do tachograph symbols mean?", this guide explains the digital tachograph symbols meaning, the legal status behind each icon, and common mistakes to avoid.

The four main activity symbols

All digital tachographs record four activity types. The artwork can vary by brand (VDO, Stoneridge), but the meanings are consistent across the EU and UK. Driving is automatic when the vehicle moves; the others must be selected manually.

Symbol When it is shown Legal status
Driving
 Automatically whenever the vehicle is in motion. Counts as driving time under EU/GB drivers' hours rules. Contributes to the 4 hours 30 minutes driving limit before a break is due, and the 9/10-hour daily driving limits.
Other work
 Physical or non-driving work such as loading, unloading, cleaning, paperwork, and your vehicle walkaround check. Counts as working time under the Road Transport Working Time Regulations. Does not reset the driving-time break requirement.
Availability
 Waiting time where you must be available but not working — for example, a second driver while the vehicle moves, or depot queueing where the end time is known. Recorded as a Period of Availability (POA). Not working time and not rest. Does not count towards breaks and does not reset the 4h30 driving limit.
Rest / Break
 Breaks and rest periods when you are free of all work. Only this activity satisfies drivers' hours break requirements. A 45-minute break (which can be split 15+30) resets the 4h30 driving limit. Used for daily and weekly rest.

Tip: Many digital units default to Other work when the vehicle stops. Manually change to Rest/Break if you are taking a statutory break — otherwise your break will not be recorded.

Card and crew symbols

Driver card insertion and removal

Card icons show insertion and removal with an arrow. Slot 1 is for the main driver; Slot 2 is for a second driver.

At card insertion, enter your start country and confirm any activities done away from the vehicle (e.g. other work). On removal, confirm your end country. Failing to complete these steps is an infringement.

Single- and multi-driver (crew) mode

A crew or "two heads" icon indicates dual-manned operation. The head unit will show both drivers' activity symbols at once.

The second driver should be set to Availability when simply accompanying. If they are taking a statutory break, select Rest/Break. Daily rest cannot be taken in a moving vehicle.

Country indicators

Digital tachographs display two-letter country codes (for example GB, FR, DE) at duty start and end. Some units also print the code on activity printouts.

Always confirm the correct country when prompted. Incorrect or missing start/end countries can lead to fixed penalties.

Out-of-scope and special situations

OUT (outside the scope of EU drivers' hours)

An OUT setting marks activity outside Regulation (EC) 561/2006, such as certain exempt vehicles or operations. The display may show "OUT" or an out-of-scope symbol.

Use OUT only when you are genuinely exempt. OUT periods are stored but not counted towards EU drivers' hours. Switch back when returning to in-scope work. Domestic drivers' hours rules may still apply.

Ferry and train mode

A ferry or train symbol is used to record a daily rest that is interrupted by a sea or rail crossing. You may interrupt a regular daily rest up to twice for embarking and disembarking.

You must still achieve the required daily rest duration (typically 11 hours, or 9 hours when reduced). Follow the unit prompts to mark the crossing and keep evidence (tickets) with you.

Smart tachographs and border crossings

Smart Tachograph versions (especially Generation 2 V2 on newer vehicles) can record border crossings automatically via GNSS. You may see prompts or a globe/location icon.

Continue to enter your start and end country when required. Automatic border logging does not replace these manual entries.

Warning symbols you must know

Warning icons vary by brand, but the following alerts are common. If you see a triangle, bell, or exclamation with text, check your screen or printout for details.

  • Overspeed: A recorded speed above the legal threshold for a set period. Slow down immediately. Overspeed events are stored and may be inspected by DVSA.
  • Break due / driving time exceeded: You are approaching or have exceeded the 4h30 driving limit, or the daily/weekly driving limits. Take a Rest/Break now.
  • No card / motion without card: The vehicle has moved without a driver card in Slot 1. Stop and insert your card. Motion without card is a serious infringement.
  • Card error or conflict: The card is unreadable, expired, locked, or in the wrong slot. Reinsert or replace the card and contact your transport office if it persists.
  • Printer out of paper: Replace the roll before you need a printout for roadside checks.
  • Sensor or head unit fault: A malfunction warning (often a spanner or fault icon). Report immediately and have the tachograph checked by an approved workshop.
  • Calibration due: The unit is due for periodic inspection or recalibration. Book the workshop to avoid penalties.
  • Download due: A reminder that company or vehicle data are due for download. Ensure downloads are completed within legal timeframes.

Common mistakes drivers make with symbols

  • Leaving the unit on "Other work" during a break: This will not count as a statutory break. Always switch to Rest/Break.
  • Choosing Availability instead of Rest: POA does not reset the driving-time limit or count as a WTD break.
  • Forgetting manual entries: Not adding activities away from the vehicle (e.g. pre-shift work) leads to gaps and potential infringements.
  • Using OUT incorrectly: Setting OUT to hide driving is illegal and easily detected in data analysis.
  • Second driver not set correctly: In crew mode, the co-driver should be on Availability, or on Rest if taking a break. Do not leave them on Other work by default.
  • Missing country entries: Skipping start or end country prompts creates avoidable non-compliance.
  • Not using ferry/train mode: Rest may be treated as interrupted incorrectly if you do not mark a crossing.
  • Removing the card too soon: Wait until the unit finishes saving data and confirms end country.

FAQ: tachograph activity symbols

What do tachograph symbols mean?

They show your current activity: Driving, Other work, Availability, and Rest/Break. The four main pictograms appear on every digital tachograph display. They are accompanied by icons for card insertion and removal, multi-driver mode, ferry/train crossings, country codes, and warnings such as overspeed or break due.

What is the difference between "Other work" and "Availability"?

Other work is working time (loading, paperwork, vehicle checks). Availability is waiting time where you must be ready to work but are not doing any. Availability is not working time and not rest, and it does not count as a break.

Can Availability count as a break under drivers' hours?

No. Only Rest/Break satisfies the 45-minute break required after 4h30 of driving. Availability (POA) does not reset the driving limit and does not count towards Working Time Directive breaks.

What should I do if I forgot to switch to Rest during a break?

Change to Rest as soon as you realise. If appropriate, make a printout, add a written note explaining the error, and sign it. You cannot overwrite recorded activities, but a signed note may help your operator explain an isolated mistake at a roadside check.
