Digital tachograph: how to correctly download, evaluate and archive data

By Truck Data Technology Updated April 2026 10 min read

If you operate a truck, bus or — from July 2026 — a van over 2.5 t in international transport, a digital tachograph is part of your daily reality. Whether you drive yourself or manage a fleet, you have several legal obligations that you need to get right. This guide walks you through the entire process.

Why downloading alone is not enough

Many operators assume that simply downloading data from the tachograph is sufficient. It is not. EU Regulation 561/2006 requires you to:

  • Download data from driver cards and the tachograph unit at legally prescribed intervals
  • Evaluate the data — check driving times, breaks and rest periods against legal limits
  • Archive the data for at least 1 year (we recommend 3–5 years)

Inspectors don't just check whether data has been downloaded — they check whether it has been properly evaluated. Without dedicated software, you are exposed to significant fines even if the data exists on your hard drive.

Tip

What you need to comply

To fulfil your obligations correctly, you need three things working together:

  1. A company card — inserted in the tachograph whenever you download vehicle unit data. Without it, the tachograph will refuse to release the data. The card is issued by your national transport authority and is valid for 5 years.
  2. A download device — such as the Download Box II S, a handheld device that reads data from both the tachograph unit and driver cards via a direct connection.
  3. Evaluation software — such as TAGRA, which automatically analyses the downloaded files, flags violations and generates reports accepted by enforcement authorities.

Step-by-step: the correct process

1. Download driver card data

At least every 28 days, each driver card must be read using a download device. The data is stored as .DDD or .C1B files — formats that cannot be opened with a standard viewer. You need specialist software to read them.

2. Download vehicle unit data

At least every 90 days, connect your download device to the vehicle's tachograph unit with the company card inserted. The data is extracted and stored as .V1B or .DDD files.

3. Import into TAGRA and evaluate

Import the downloaded files into TAGRA. The software automatically evaluates:

  • Driving times, breaks and rest periods against EU 561/2006 and AETR
  • Working time under the Working Time Directive (including shifts, overtime and night work)
  • Any violations, with clear reports you can share with inspectors

4. Archive

TAGRA archives all data automatically. You don't need to manage separate file systems — everything is searchable and retrievable at any time.

What inspectors actually check

Road transport inspectors — including Police, Customs and Labour Inspectorates — don't just look at raw data. They check:

  • Whether data has been downloaded within the legal deadlines
  • Whether driving times and rest periods comply with EU 561/2006
  • Whether working time has been correctly evaluated under labour law
  • Whether the archiving is complete and accessible
Important

TAGRA is actively used by national Police forces, Customs Administrations, Labour Inspectorates and Ministries of Transport across Europe. When you evaluate your data with TAGRA, you get exactly the same output format that inspectors use — eliminating any ambiguity.

G2V2 smart tachographs

Since 2023, all new vehicles must be fitted with second-generation smart tachographs (G2V2). These record GPS position data at regular intervals and require specific tools and software to read correctly. TAGRA fully supports G2V2 data including extended position records.

From July 2026: vans in international transport

A significant legislative change affects operators of light commercial vehicles of 2.5–3.5 t used in international transport. From 1 July 2026, these vehicles must be fitted with a digital tachograph and their operators must comply with the same download, evaluation and archiving requirements as heavier vehicles.
