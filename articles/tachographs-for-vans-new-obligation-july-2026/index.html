Tachographs for vans: new obligation from July 2026

Truck Data TechnologyUpdated April 202610 min read

From 1 July 2026, the tachograph obligation extends to light commercial vehicles with a gross weight of 2.5 t and above, if used for international carriage of goods for hire or reward or for cabotage* within the EU. For vehicles over 3.5 t this obligation already applies today.

Effective from
1 Jul 2026
Extension of the obligation to selected vans in international transport.
From weight
2.5 t
Applies to vehicles in the 2.5–3.5 t range when conditions are met.
Tachograph type
G2 v2
Smart tachograph 2nd generation is required for newly registered vehicles.

In addition to the tachograph, drivers in international transport need a driver card, and the operator needs a company card.

Every operator has the following obligations regarding tachograph and driver card data:

  • Download — driver card data at least every 28 days, vehicle unit at least every 90 days.
  • Evaluate and archive — check compliance with driving and rest rules; archive data for at least 1 year.

Quick test — does this apply to you?

Two questions that usually decide.

Step 1
Does the van's registration document show a gross weight above 2.5 t?
If the vehicle is below 2.5 t, the change typically does not affect you.
Step 2
Do you carry goods across borders for hire or reward, or perform cabotage* in another EU country?
For vehicles above 3.5 t, you are already required to use a tachograph today.

If both conditions apply — the change almost certainly affects you.

Who is affected

  • hauliers and operators in international freight transport between EU countries
  • companies performing cabotage (domestic transport within another EU country)

Who is NOT affected

  • purely domestic transport (within one country)
  • private, non-commercial journeys
  • international transport on own account (e.g. going across a border to collect materials for your own company), where driving is not the driver's main profession
  • vehicles below 2.5 t gross weight
  • vehicles designed to carry up to nine persons including the driver
Practical example — own account
You are not a carrier and you cross a border to pick up goods for your own business? If the transport is not for hire or reward and driving is not your main profession, the own-account exemption may apply for vans of 2.5–3.5 t. But the exact situation and the weight (including any trailer) always matters.
For reference: exceptions from Regulation 561/2006 and the full text of Regulation (EC) No 561/2006.

Own-account at an inspection

Inspectors typically don't just ask whether someone paid you — they check whether you can demonstrate clearly that your company is carrying its own goods for its own purpose. There is no single universal form. Logical documentation is what counts:

  • Document for the goods: invoice, delivery note, order confirmation or receipt.
  • Purpose of the journey: a brief internal note for the driver (from where, to where and why).
  • Link driver–company–vehicle: employment contract or driver authorisation and vehicle documentation.

In practice: the driver should be able to briefly say what they are carrying, where from, where to and for whom. That usually decides the outcome.

What will be required

  • installation of a smart tachograph 2nd generation (G2v2) — older types are not sufficient
  • use of a driver card for each driver in international transport
  • company card for the operator (authorisation and data download from the vehicle)
  • regular downloading of data from the tachograph and driver card
  • evaluation and archiving of data in line with the legislation

What to do as soon as possible

  • Start with a fleet analysis and book installation appointments at an authorised workshop well in advance.
  • Apply for cards early: driver card for drivers, company card for the operator.
  • Set up an internal procedure and train staff who plan and manage transport.
  • Get a download device and TAGRA software for evaluation and archiving.

FAQ — frequently asked questions

Who will be subject to the tachograph obligation from 1 July 2026?

The obligation applies to vans with a gross weight of 2.5–3.5 t used for international carriage of goods for hire or reward or for cabotage within the EU. For vehicles above 3.5 t, the tachograph is already mandatory today.

Does a van need a tachograph if it only travels within one country?

No. If you use a van below 3.5 t exclusively for domestic transport (within one country only), the tachograph obligation does not apply after July 2026. The change applies specifically to international commercial transport and cabotage.

Does the obligation apply to vehicles with a trailer, if the van itself is below 2.5 t?

Yes — the decisive factor is the combined gross weight of the combination (vehicle + trailer). If the van itself weighs 2 t but you attach a trailer and the total combined weight exceeds 2.5 t, the same rules apply to you when carrying goods internationally for hire or reward.

What do I need to sort out besides installing the tachograph?

You will need to arrange regular downloading of data (from both the tachograph and the driver card), their legal evaluation and archiving, obtaining the required cards, and compliance with driving and rest period rules. We recommend Download Box devices for downloading and TAGRA software for evaluation and archiving.

How often must data be downloaded?

Driver card data at least every 28 days and vehicle unit data at least every 90 days. With the right equipment this takes just a few seconds.

Which cards are needed?

Drivers need a personal driver card. The company needs a company card, used for authorisation and secure downloading of vehicle data.

I'm not a carrier. I occasionally cross a border to collect goods for my own company. Do I need a tachograph?

The decisive factors are the gross weight of the vehicle (including any trailer) and the exact purpose of the transport. If it is not transport for hire or reward and driving is not your main profession, the own-account exemption usually applies. We recommend assessing each situation individually — contact us.

How is own-account transport demonstrated in practice?

There is no single universal form. A document for the goods (invoice, delivery note, order), a brief explanation of the journey purpose, and the link between driver, company and vehicle (authorisation, employment relationship, vehicle documentation) are usually sufficient.

Is there an exemption for tradespeople?

Yes — the so-called craftsman exemption. If driving is not your main activity and you are carrying only materials, instruments or equipment that you need for your profession, you generally do not need a tachograph.

What is cabotage?

Cabotage is the carriage of goods within one EU country performed by a carrier based in a different EU country. Example: with a Czech van you unload goods in Hamburg and then take another shipment from Hamburg to Berlin.

I'm renting a van. Who is responsible for the tachograph?

If you rent a van (above 2.5 t) for international commercial transport, it must be fitted with a tachograph. The responsibility for correct data downloading and compliance lies with you as the operator, not the rental company. Remember to log into the tachograph with your company card before the journey.

Can I install the tachograph myself?

No. Installation, activation and regular verification (calibration) must be performed exclusively by an authorised metrology workshop.

What fines apply if I don't have a tachograph in my van after 1 July 2026?

A missing tachograph, driving without a card or failing to download data are treated as very serious violations in all EU countries. Fines can reach thousands of euros and in many cases the vehicle may be stopped on the spot. Repeated violations can result in loss of the operator's good repute.

* Cabotage is the carriage of goods within one EU country performed by a carrier based in a different EU country. Example: you unload in Hamburg and then carry a shipment to Berlin.
